Dr. Verghese Kurien

The man who brought the white revolution

Here's a timeline of Dr. Verghese Kurien's life:

  • 1921 - Born on November 26 in Kozhikode, Kerala, India.
  • 1940 - Graduates in Physics from Loyola College, Chennai.
  • 1943 - Obtains a degree in Mechanical Engineering from the College of Engineering, Guindy, Chennai.
  • 1946 - Receives a government scholarship to study Dairy Engineering at Michigan State University.
  • 1948 - Earns a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering with a focus on dairy engineering.
  • 1949 - Returns to India and is deputed to the Government Creamery in Anand, Gujarat.
  • 1950 - Begins working with Tribhuvandas Patel to establish the cooperative model in Anand, leading to the formation of the Amul brand.
  • 1955 - Sets up the first milk powder plant in India, processing buffalo milk instead of cow's milk, a significant innovation.

"Dr. Verghese Kurien's pioneering efforts in dairy development stand as a powerful example of how dedication, vision, and innovation can transform lives and uplift communities."
-- Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
